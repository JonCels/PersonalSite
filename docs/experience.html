Work & Role Description:

I worked at Roche Canada for 4 months during my summer break in 2020, which was fully remote due to it being the height of the pandemic. My team consisted of me and another co-op student as developers, a project manager who we reported to, as well as a scrum master, business analyst, and professional UX designer. Our project was to create Skill Explorer, which you can learn about on my Project page.

It took a few weeks to get up and running properly, as software installations, proper understanding of the project, and learning new technologies all took time. I had never created a web application before, and was meant to use all of JavaScript, Vue.js, and MySQL for the first time. After doing a ton of research in my personal time and learning how to use the frameworks and set up the project, we got started with the real project development.

We worked in sprints, with each sprint lasting a week with a predetermined number of tasks in scope for that sprint. Every morning we had a daily scrum, where I would share the work I completed and what I was doing next. To facilitate project management and task completion we used Jira.

This experience allowed me to work alongside a number of experienced professionals, like a UX designer and business analyst. The UX designer used Figma mockups, showing us their vision and allowing us to come to a decision on the look and feel of the application. The designs then acted as a guide for my development. When designing the backend, I also got to work alongside a business analyst who was familiar with MySQL and database design. They helped me learn the technology as well as structure the database around the business.

In the end we completed the application, but were unable to see it deployed into a production environment before we left, as another team took over the security and code review for production code. Ultimately the experience was invaluable to me, as this was my first exposure to web application development, and taught me a new framework in Vue, taught me Node, Express, and Axios, and taught me MySQL, all from scratch. Working alongside my small team also enabled me to focus purely on the developmental aspect of the project. Overall this was a great experience as the work, people, and environment were fantastic.
